Understanding DORA Metrics

DORA (DevOps Research and Assessment) metrics are the industry-standard framework for measuring software delivery performance. These four key metrics—Deployment Frequency, Lead Time for Changes, Mean Time to Recovery, and Change Failure Rate—provide quantifiable insights into your team's DevOps capabilities and identify areas for improvement.

1. Deployment Frequency

Measures how frequently your organization successfully releases code to production or end users.

2. Lead Time for Changes

Measures the time from code commit to successful deployment in production.

3. Mean Time to Recovery

Measures how quickly your team can restore service when a service incident or defect occurs in production.

4. Change Failure Rate

Measures the percentage of deployments that cause failures in production requiring immediate remediation.
